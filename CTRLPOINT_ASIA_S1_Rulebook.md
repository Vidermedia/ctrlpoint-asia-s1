# CTRLPOINT ASIA Season 1 — Rulebook

**Tournament Operator:** Vidermedia (ViderGG)
**Event Name:** CTRLPOINT ASIA Season 1
**Game:** Counter-Strike 2
**Document Version:** 1.0
**Publication Date:** May 10, 2026
**Publication Platform:** https://github.com/Vidermedia/ctrlpoint-asia-s1

This Rulebook governs match procedures, conduct, tournament administration, and player eligibility for CTRLPOINT ASIA Season 1. For tournament structure, invitation procedures, prize pool, and qualifier eligibility, refer to the [Additional Information document](https://github.com/Vidermedia/ctrlpoint-asia-s1/blob/main/additional-information.md).

By participating in CTRLPOINT ASIA Season 1, all teams, players, coaches, and staff agree to abide by this Rulebook in full.

---

## 1. Roster Composition

### 1.1 Roster Requirements

Each Roster must consist of:

- **5 Core Players** — the active competitive lineup
- **1 Optional Coach**
- **1 Optional Substitute Player**

The Coach and Substitute may be the same person.

### 1.2 Roster Submission

All Rosters must be submitted to Tournament Operator via the official portal by the Roster Submission Deadline. Submissions must include:

- Full legal name of each player
- Steam ID for each player
- FACEIT username for each player
- Country of citizenship for each player
- Player handle/nickname used in-game and on broadcast
- Coach and Substitute information (if applicable)
- Organization details and primary point of contact

### 1.3 Roster Lock

After the Roster Submission Deadline:

- Direct VRS Invitees may not modify their Core Lineup
- Open Qualifier advancers may not modify their Core Lineup between Open Qualifier completion and Stage 1
- Stage 1 advancers may not modify their Core Lineup between Stage 1 conclusion and Stage 2

### 1.4 Invite Eligibility

Rosters accepting a Direct VRS Invite must include at least 3 of the 5 Athletes who were on the Roster on the Invite Date, and these 3 Athletes must play in each match.

### 1.5 Substitution Rules

Each Roster is permitted **one (1) substitution per stage** using their registered Substitute.

Substitutions may occur:
- **Between matches:** For any reason
- **Between maps within a series:** Only in case of medical emergency, with admin approval

Once substituted in, the Substitute may continue playing until the team voluntarily reverts to the Core Lineup. Once reverted, no further substitutions are permitted for the remainder of that stage. If a player cannot continue and no further substitution is allowed, the team must play shorthanded or forfeit.

### 1.6 One Roster Per Organization

Only one Roster per organization may participate. If an organization holds multiple Rosters, only the first to accept an invitation or qualify is eligible for the tournament.

### 1.7 Player Age

All participants must be 16 years of age or older at the time of registration or invite acceptance. Tournament Operator may require proof of age.

## 2. Tournament Format

### 2.1 Stage 1: Swiss Group Stage

- **Participants:** 16 teams (10 Direct VRS Invites + 6 Open Qualifier advancers)
- **Format:** 5-round Swiss system
- **Match Format:** All matches Best-of-3 (BO3)
- **Advancement:** A Roster reaching 3 wins advances to Stage 2
- **Elimination:** A Roster reaching 3 losses is eliminated from the tournament

### 2.2 Stage 1 Round Pairings

- **Round 1:** Initial pairings determined by seeding (Section 2.3). Highest seed plays lowest seed within each opening bracket pair.
- **Rounds 2–5:** Teams paired by record. Within each record group, pairings determined by Buchholz tiebreaker (cumulative opponent strength).

### 2.3 Stage 1 Initial Seeding

Seeding for Round 1 is determined by the June 2026 Asia VRS publication.

- **Seeds 1–10:** The 10 Direct VRS Invites, ranked in descending order of Asia VRS rank.
- **Seeds 11–16:** The 6 Open Qualifier advancers. Unranked Rosters are seeded below all VRS-ranked Rosters. Among Open Qualifier advancers, seeding is determined by:
  1. Sub-regional Open Qualifier finishing position
  2. Asia VRS rank if applicable
  3. Random draw if all above are equal

### 2.4 Stage 1 Tiebreakers

For pairing purposes in Rounds 2–5 and for final standings:

1. Match record (wins-losses)
2. Buchholz score (sum of defeated opponents' match wins)
3. Map differential
4. Round differential
5. Initial seeding (Asia VRS rank)

### 2.5 Stage 2: Playoffs

- **Participants:** 16 teams (8 Direct VRS Invites + 8 Stage 1 advancers)
- **Format:** Single-elimination bracket
- **Match Format:** Best-of-3 (BO3) for Round of 16, Quarterfinals, and Semifinals; Best-of-5 (BO5) for Grand Final

### 2.6 Stage 2 Seeding

- **Seeds 1–8:** Stage 2 Direct VRS Invites, ranked in descending order of Asia VRS rank.
- **Seeds 9–16:** Stage 1 advancers, seeded by Stage 1 final standings (per Section 2.4).

### 2.7 Stage 2 Bracket Pairings

Standard single-elimination bracket pairings:

| Round of 16 | Pairing |
|-------------|---------|
| Match 1 | Seed 1 vs Seed 16 |
| Match 2 | Seed 8 vs Seed 9 |
| Match 3 | Seed 4 vs Seed 13 |
| Match 4 | Seed 5 vs Seed 12 |
| Match 5 | Seed 3 vs Seed 14 |
| Match 6 | Seed 6 vs Seed 11 |
| Match 7 | Seed 2 vs Seed 15 |
| Match 8 | Seed 7 vs Seed 10 |

Quarterfinal, Semifinal, and Grand Final pairings are determined by bracket progression.

## 3. Match Procedures

### 3.1 Game Version

All matches will be played on the latest available version of Counter-Strike 2 at the time of the match. Players whose game is not updated will be unable to join the server.

### 3.2 Map Pool

The Valve Active Duty map pool will be used for all matches. As of the publication date, the Active Duty pool is:

1. Mirage
2. Dust 2
3. Anubis
4. Ancient
5. Inferno
6. Nuke
7. Overpass

If Valve modifies the Active Duty pool during the tournament, the modified pool will be used. Any such change will be communicated to all Rosters at least 48 hours before its effective date.

### 3.3 Server Locations

| Stage | Server |
|-------|--------|
| Oceania Open Qualifier | Sydney or Melbourne |
| West Asia Open Qualifier | Singapore (SGP) |
| East Asia Open Qualifier | Singapore (SGP) |
| Stage 1 (Swiss) | Singapore (SGP) |
| Stage 2 (Playoffs) | Singapore (SGP) |

Server selection is non-negotiable and applies equally to all teams within each stage.

### 3.4 Anti-Cheat

FACEIT Anti-Cheat (FACEIT AC) is mandatory for all matches. All players must install and run FACEIT AC for every match. Failure to comply may result in match forfeiture.

### 3.5 Match Platform

All matches are conducted on FACEIT.

## 4. Pre-Match Process

### 4.1 Voting Time Limits

Teams have 5 minutes total for the pick/ban process per match. Each individual ban or pick has a 90-second time limit. Failure to act within 90 seconds results in automatic action by the system.

### 4.2 Coin Toss

A coin toss determines the higher seed for the pre-match process. The higher seed chooses to be Team A or Team B.

### 4.3 Server Selection — Oceania Open Qualifier (2 server locations)

For Oceania Open Qualifier matches with Sydney and Melbourne available:

1. Team A bans 1 server location
2. Remaining server location is selected

### 4.4 Server Selection — All Other Matches (1 server location)

For all West Asia, East Asia, Stage 1, and Stage 2 matches: Server is locked to Singapore (SGP). No server selection process required.

### 4.5 Pick/Ban — Best-of-1 (Open Qualifier early rounds)

1. Higher seed (Team A) bans 2 maps
2. Team B bans 3 maps
3. Team A bans 1 map
4. Team B chooses starting side on the remaining map

### 4.6 Pick/Ban — Best-of-3 (Open Qualifier finals, Stage 1, Stage 2 R16/QF/SF)

1. Team A bans 1 map
2. Team B bans 1 map
3. Team A picks Map 1
4. Team B chooses starting side on Map 1
5. Team B picks Map 2
6. Team A chooses starting side on Map 2
7. Team B bans 1 map
8. Team A bans 1 map
9. Remaining map is Map 3. Team B chooses starting side on Map 3.

### 4.7 Pick/Ban — Best-of-5 (Grand Final)

1. Team A bans 1 map
2. Team B bans 1 map
3. Team A picks Map 1
4. Team B chooses starting side on Map 1
5. Team B picks Map 2
6. Team A chooses starting side on Map 2
7. Team A picks Map 3
8. Team B chooses starting side on Map 3
9. Team B picks Map 4
10. Team A chooses starting side on Map 4
11. Remaining map is Map 5. Team B chooses starting side on Map 5.

## 5. Match Conduct

### 5.1 Ready-Up Times

- **Between series:** 15 minutes maximum
- **Between maps within a series:** 10 minutes maximum

Failure to ready up within these windows may result in forfeit at Tournament Operator's discretion.

### 5.2 Pause Allowances

- **Tech pauses (`!tech`):** Each team is permitted 10 minutes of total tech pause time per match
- **Tactical pauses (`!pause`):** Each team is permitted three (3) 30-second tactical pauses per map

In online matches, players are responsible for securing their internet connections and preparing a backup solution in case of technical outage.

### 5.3 Coach and Voice Communication

Only the 5 players and the Coach may participate in the team's voice communication channel during a match. The Coach may communicate with players at all times during the match but may not join the game server. No other team staff are permitted in the voice channel or game server, except for tournament admins, who may be present in both for integrity purposes.

### 5.4 POV Streams

Personal POV streaming by individual players is not permitted on any platform during matches. Teams may share POV privately with their Coach for in-match communication purposes only.

### 5.5 Voice Recording

For competitive integrity, all team voice communications (Discord, TeamSpeak, or equivalent) will be recorded during matches. Recordings may be reviewed by tournament administration to ensure rule compliance.

### 5.6 Streaming Delay

Tournament Operator may apply a broadcast delay to the official stream for competitive integrity purposes. Teams may not access the official broadcast during their own matches.

## 6. Forbidden In-Game Actions

The following are strictly prohibited and may result in penalties up to and including disqualification:

### 6.1 Exploits and Bugs

- Use of any form of external script (except buy/toggle scripts permitted by Valve)
- Exploitation of bugs that change game principles (e.g., spawn bugs)
- Moving through walls, floors, or roofs
- Sky-walking
- Pixel-walking (sitting or standing on invisible map edges)
- Boosting in locations where textures, walls, ceilings, or floors become transparent or penetrable

### 6.2 Bomb-Related Violations

- Planting bombs in locations where they cannot be defused
- Planting bombs in locations not touching a solid object
- Planting bombs in locations not making the normal "beeping" sound
- Defusing bombs through solid objects

### 6.3 In-Game Display

- Use of in-game items (skins, name tags, sprays) containing offensive, discriminatory, or sponsor-promotional content
- Display of racist, discriminatory, or hateful symbols, language, or imagery in-game, in player names, or in usernames
- Modification of agent skins
- `cl_showfps` values other than 0 or 1

### 6.4 Custom Files

Custom game files or data are not permitted during official matches.

### 6.5 New Position Reporting

Any new position or technique that may be considered an exploit must be reported to tournament officials before use.

## 7. Sanctions and Penalties

### 7.1 Warnings

A team may receive a warning for:

- Refusing to follow instructions of tournament officials
- Use of abusive language
- Unsporting behavior

### 7.2 Penalties

A team may receive penalties (including round losses, map forfeitures, or match forfeitures) for:

- Repeated refusal to follow instructions of tournament officials
- Late arrival to scheduled matches
- Repeated use of abusive language
- Use of any unlawful or dishonest practices
- Misleading or deceiving any tournament official
- Violation of Section 6 (Forbidden In-Game Actions)

### 7.3 Disqualification

A Roster may be disqualified for:

- Active VAC ban discovered on any player
- Match-fixing or betting-related violations
- Repeated rule violations after warnings and penalties
- Severe Code of Conduct violations
- Failure to participate in scheduled matches without valid reason

### 7.4 Final Authority

Depending on the seriousness of the behavior, tournament officials will set the final rules and may apply stiffer penalties as necessary. Verbal offense includes vulgar language, offensive words, and excessive arguments with tournament officials.

## 8. Code of Conduct

### 8.1 Professional Behavior

All participants — players, coaches, managers, and staff — are expected to maintain professional conduct toward:

- Tournament officials
- Opposing teams and their staff
- Sponsors and partners
- Broadcasters, casters, and production staff
- Viewers and the broader community

### 8.2 Prohibited Conduct

The following conduct is strictly prohibited:

- Verbal abuse of any tournament representative or participant
- Use of discriminatory, hateful, or harassing language or imagery
- Repeated unsporting behavior
- Misleading or deceiving tournament officials
- Public defamation of opponents, officials, or the tournament

### 8.3 Bets and Match Fixing

Players, coaches, managers, and staff associated with participating Rosters are prohibited from:

- Placing bets on any CTRLPOINT ASIA Season 1 matches
- Attempting to influence match outcomes for non-competitive reasons
- Sharing confidential strategic or roster information with external parties

Violation results in immediate disqualification, prize forfeiture, and a minimum 2-year ban from CTRLPOINT events.

### 8.4 VAC Bans

No player with an active Valve Anti-Cheat (VAC) ban in any Counter-Strike title is permitted to participate. Discovery of a VAC-banned player on a Roster results in:

- Immediate Roster disqualification
- Forfeiture of all winnings
- Ineligibility for future CTRLPOINT events

### 8.5 Team Names and Logos

Team names and logos must not contain offensive, discriminatory, or otherwise inappropriate content. Tournament Operator reserves the right to require modification of team branding for broadcast purposes.

### 8.6 Player Names and Avatars

In-game player names and avatars must not contain offensive, discriminatory, or otherwise inappropriate content.

## 10. Disputes and Refereeing

### 10.1 Dispute Submission

Disputes regarding rule interpretation, match results, or competitive integrity must be submitted to tournament administration within 30 minutes of the relevant match conclusion via the official Discord support channel or sm@vidermedia.live.

### 10.2 Decision Authority

Decisions by tournament administration are final and not subject to appeal. Rulings will be made consistent with the spirit of these regulations.

### 10.3 Unforeseen Situations

For situations not explicitly covered by this Rulebook, Tournament Operator and tournament officials will issue rulings consistent with the spirit of these regulations. Such rulings will be made transparently and fairly to all affected parties.

### 10.4 Rule Modifications

Tournament Operator reserves the right to modify this Rulebook to preserve fair play and competitive integrity. All modifications will be:

- Published with version history at the GitHub repository
- Communicated to participating Rosters at least 48 hours in advance where feasible
- Logged with date, scope, and rationale

Material modifications occurring after a stage has begun will only apply to subsequent stages, except where required to address active integrity concerns.

## 11. Match Forfeiture and No-Shows

### 11.1 No-Show Definition

A team is considered a no-show if it fails to ready up within 15 minutes of the scheduled match start time without prior approved delay.

### 11.2 No-Show Penalty

A no-show may result in:

- Forfeit of the affected match
- Forfeit of map advantage if applicable
- Tournament Operator discretion for further penalties on repeat offenses

### 11.3 Player Availability

In the event a player cannot continue mid-match (technical issue, medical emergency), the team may use their Substitute per Section 1.5. If no Substitute is available, the team must play shorthanded or forfeit.

### 11.4 Connection Issues

Players are responsible for their own internet connections. Tournament Operator is not responsible for connection issues on the player's side. Tech pauses (Section 5.2) provide reasonable accommodation for transient issues.
